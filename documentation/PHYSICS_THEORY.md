### 1. General Relativity (GR)
* **Explanation**: Gravity is modeled not as a Newtonian force, but as the geometric distortion of 4D spacetime fabric by mass and energy. Massive objects create geodesic paths that other matter slides along.
* **Mathematical Model**: Einstein Field Equations
  $$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$
  Where $G_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor, $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric tensor, $T_{\mu\nu}$ is the stress-energy tensor, $G$ is the Newtonian gravitational constant, and $\Lambda$ is the Cosmological Constant.
* **Consensus/Evidence**: 100%. Validated by gravitational lensing, orbit precession, and gravitational wave detections.
* **References**: 
  * Einstein, A. (1916). *Die Grundlage der allgemeinen RelativitĂ¤tstheorie*. Annalen der Physik, 49(7).

### 2. Spacetime Curvature
* **Explanation**: Spacetime geometry deviates from flat Minkowski space near heavy bodies. Free-falling particles trace geodesics representing the straightest possible paths in a curved manifold.
* **Mathematical Model**: Geodesic Deviation & Riemann Curvature Tensor
  $$\frac{d^2 x^\mu}{d\tau^2} + \Gamma^\mu_{\alpha\beta} \frac{dx^\alpha}{d\tau} \frac{dx^\beta}{d\tau} = 0$$
  Where $\Gamma^\mu_{\alpha\beta}$ is the Christoffel symbol of the second kind.
* **Consensus/Evidence**: 100%. Verified by satellite tracking, atomic clock time dilation, and Mercury's orbital perihelion precession.
* **References**:
  * Carroll, S. M. (2004). *Spacetime and Geometry: An Introduction to General Relativity*. Addison-Wesley.

### 3. Vacuum Energy
* **Explanation**: According to Quantum Field Theory (QFT), a vacuum is not empty space but a dynamic ground state filled with zero-point electromagnetic fluctuations, where virtual particles continuously emerge and annihilate.
* **Mathematical Model**: Quantum Ground State Summation
  $$E_0 = \sum_{k} \frac{1}{2} \hbar \omega_k$$
  Where $\hbar$ is the reduced Planck constant and $\omega_k$ is the frequency of mode $k$.
* **Consensus/Evidence**: 95%. Indirect verification from atomic Lamb shifts and high-precision Casimir plate deflection.
* **References**:
  * Milonni, P. W. (1994). *The Quantum Vacuum: An Introduction to Quantum Electrodynamics*. Academic Press.

### 4. Casimir Effect
* **Explanation**: An attractive physical pressure between two parallel, conducting, uncharged metal plates in a vacuum. The narrow gap excludes electromagnetic virtual wave modes whose wavelengths do not divide the spacing, lowering the inner energy density relative to the outer boundary pressure.
* **Mathematical Model**: Casimir Force Per Unit Area
  $$\frac{F}{A} = -\frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{240 d^4}$$
  Where $d$ is the plate separation distance and $A$ is the plate surface area.
* **Consensus/Evidence**: 100%. Measured with sub-nanonewton accuracy using atomic force microscopes in ultra-high vacuum conditions.
* **References**:
  * Casimir, H. B. (1948). *On the attraction between two perfectly conducting plates*. Proc. K. Ned. Akad. Wet.

Experimental physics modules have theoretical backing but lack rigorous replication or direct observation.

### 5. Quantum Gravity (QG)
* **Explanation**: Frameworks designed to reconcile General Relativity with Quantum Mechanics. General relativity treats spacetime as a smooth, continuous manifold, while quantum mechanics describes discrete, quantized states.
* **Mathematical Model**: Wheeler-DeWitt Equation
  $$\hat{H} | \Psi \rangle = 0$$
  Where $\hat{H}$ is the quantized Hamiltonian constraint and $| \Psi \rangle$ is the wave function of the universe.
* **Status**: High scientific interest. Competing models include String Theory and Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG).
* **References**:
  * Rovelli, C. (2004). *Loop Quantum Gravity*. Living Reviews in Relativity.

### 6. Dark Energy
* **Explanation**: A pressure-negative fluid permeating the cosmos. Although its astronomical existence is proven, its exact physical origin is unknown, placing it in the experimental/unsolved category.
* **Mathematical Model**: Equation of State
  $$P = w \rho c^2 \quad (\text{where } w \approx -1)$$
  Where $P$ is pressure and $\rho$ is energy density.
* **Status**: Confirmed astronomically; physical nature is completely open.
* **References**:
  * Riess, A. G., et al. (1998). *Observational evidence from supernovae for an accelerating universe*. Astronomical Journal.

### 8. Negative Energy Density
* **Explanation**: Local region where the energy density of space is less than zero. While microscopic quantum states allow brief negative densities (like the Casimir gap), macroscopic stabilization is unproven.
* **Mathematical Model**: Weak Energy Condition Violation
  $$T_{\mu\nu} u^\mu u^\nu < 0$$
  Where $u^\mu$ is a time-like vector.
* **Status**: Speculative at engineering scales.
* **References**:
  * Ford, L. H., & Roman, T. A. (1996). *Restrictions on negative energy density in quantum field theory*. Phys. Rev. D.

### 9. Exotic Matter Concepts
* **Explanation**: Matter possessing hypothetical properties, such as negative gravitational or inertial mass. It would repel normal matter and accelerate opposite to the direction of a push.
* **Mathematical Model**: Repulsive Gravity
  $$F_g = -G \frac{M_{\text{normal}} M_{\text{negative}}}{r^2} > 0 \quad (\text{Repulsion})$$
* **Status**: Speculative. No macroscopic negative mass observations exist.
* **References**:
  * Bondi, H. (1957). *Negative Mass in General Relativity*. Reviews of Modern Physics.

### 10. Graviton Theory
* **Explanation**: The graviton is a hypothetical massless spin-2 boson mediating gravitational attraction in quantum field frameworks.
* **Mathematical Model**: Spin-2 Relativistic Field Operator
  $$\Box h_{\mu\nu} = - \kappa \left( T_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} \eta_{\mu\nu} T \right)$$
  Where $\Box$ is the D'Alembertian operator and $h_{\mu\nu}$ represents metric perturbations.
* **Status**: Widely accepted theoretically; unconfirmed experimentally due to the weak nature of individual graviton interactions.
* **References**:
  * Feynman, R. P. (1963). *Lectures on Gravitation*. Westview Press.
